The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Now we welcome you to visit the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project more commonly known as the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is located on the coastline of the city of Kosgoda in Sri Lanka. This project was established with the mission of preserving and protecting these ancient marine beings. Today it has developed into a significant conservation project as well as a world-known tourist attraction in Sri Lanka.

Visiting the Turtle Hatchery provides a unique opportunity to observe turtles in all stages of their lifecycle and even take part in releasing hatchling turtles safely to the ocean. Furthermore, all visitors can gain an understanding of the lifestyle of these marine organisms and the threats and challenges faced by them through the educational initiatives carried out by the hatchery.

Moreover, highly proficient veterinarians in the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery also provide services as a rehabilitation center for sick and injured sea turtles by providing nursing and necessary medication. These turtles are released back into the oceans once their recovery is ensured. In addition to contributing to sea turtle conservation efforts, through visiting the Turtle hatchery, visitors also develop a profound understanding of the fragility and beauty of sea turtles, which motivates them to act as advocates for their preservation.

How to reach the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery & Other Facts of Interest

Protected Sea Turtle that can be seen at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery


The Turtle Hatchery in Kosgoda acts as a rehabilitation for all five species of turtles seen in Sri Lanka, namely : The Hawksbill Turtle, The Loggerhead Turtle, The Leatherback Turtle, The Green Turtle, and The Olive Ridley Turtle. Additionally, a very rare species of Albino Sea Turtle commonly referred to as the "White Turtle" is also present in the Hatchery.
