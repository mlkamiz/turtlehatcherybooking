The Bentota Turtle Hatchery

Now we welcome you to visit the Bentota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery nestled along the picturesque shores of Bentota, Sri Lanka is a renowned conservation center committed to the protection and preservation of sea turtles.

Visitors have the chance to see the different phases of the turtle life cycle. Knowledgeable guides provide in-depth tours that enlighten visitors about the biology, behavior, and conservation initiatives related to these amazing animals. The goal of the hatchery is to educate the public about the dangers that sea turtles face, including habitat loss, pollution, and poaching, and to motivate them to take an active role in ensuring their conservation.

At the Bentota Turtle Hatchery, medical attention and a secure environment are given to sick or injured turtles so they can recuperate. These turtles are returned to the ocean after they have recovered. Individuals can aid in the preservation of sea turtles and obtain a better appreciation of their significance within marine ecosystems by visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery.

How to reach the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery & Other Facts of Interest

Protected Sea Turtle that can be seen at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery


Only four of the five turtle species that may be found in Sri Lanka—the Hawksbill, the Loggerhead, the Green, and the Olive Ridley—are rehabilitated at the Turtle Hatchery in Kosgoda. Additionally, the Hatchery is home to an extremely rare kind of albino sea turtle known as the "White Turtle.".
